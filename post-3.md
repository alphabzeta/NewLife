# A Fun Day from Old Life

I remember Kabalak Sokak in Ortakoy Neighborhood of Istanbul where I lived through my childhood from 2 to 14 years of age. Kabalak is a plant and it has always sounded funny to me with its interesting phonetics. My neighborhood Ortakoy was a lovely place in the 70s and 80s. I was one of those children of generation X whose playground were streets and my streets were Ortakoy's. 

I will write more about Ortakoy later but the day from my life in Ortakot that I will write about now is when my two friends and me became Native Americans. We, the 7 year old girls found some feathers and I told my friends to put them on back of our heads and run in the streets together like we were acting in one of western movies on Turkish TV screened on sunday mornings. Apperantly I had some leading ability at that age and I could persuade my friends to go with my crazy ways.
